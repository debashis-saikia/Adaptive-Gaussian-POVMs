# Unsharp Measurement with Adaptive Gaussian POVMs for Quantum-Inspired Image Processing

* **Author**: [Debashis Saikia](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=_s3yuzkAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao)
* **Co- Authors**: [Bikash K. Behera](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=if1XXEwAAAAJ&hl=en), [Mayukha Pal](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=2VW0NvcAAAAJ&hl=en), [Prasanta K. Panigrahi](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=sNq6fwwAAAAJ&hl=en)

-------------------------------------------------

* *Abstract*: We propose a quantum measurement-theoretic framework for probabilistic transformation of grayscale images based on adaptive positive operator-valued measures (POVMs). The intensity values of an image are embedded in a finite-dimensional Hilbert space, enabling the construction of data-adaptive measurement operators derived from Gaussian models of the image histogram. These operators define an unsharp measurement of the intensity observable, and the reconstructed image is obtained as the expectation value of the corresponding measurement outcomes. A nonlinear sharpening transformation is introduced to control the degree of measurement sharpness. We show that this transformation induces a continuous transition from unsharp POVMs to projective measurements, which is formally established and supported by experimental observations. This transition governs a trade-off between probabilistic smoothing and measurement-induced localization, leading to structured intensity transformations. The proposed framework admits an operator and channel interpretation, linking adaptive statistical modeling with the formalism of completely positive maps. Experimental results on standard images, including noisy settings, demonstrate that the method provides a flexible mechanism for controlled image transformation without explicitly performing denoising or segmentation. These results highlight the role of quantum measurement theory as a unifying framework for data-adaptive image processing.
